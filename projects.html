Winter Ember

Platforms: PC (Steam/GOG), PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox One, Xbox Series X, Nintendo Switch
Status: Released in 2022 for all the listed platforms except Switch
Engine: Unreal Engine 4
Team: Sky Machine Studios, Blowfish Studios
Role: Lead publishing developer
Contribution: Porting, optimisation, bug fixing

About:
Winter Ember is a story-driven stealth action game inspired by Thief. You play as Arthur Artorias - a sole survivor of his family's massacre who now embarks on a journey to discover the truth behind the incident. Winter Ember was started by Sky Machine Studios in 2018, then co-developed and published by Blowfish Studios. It was later given an additional patch on those platforms and is currently being developed for Nintendo Switch.

Comments:
Winter Ember was one of the first game projects I worked on professionally. It used to be managed by a more senior developer, but as I got more involved I quickly became the lead and sole developer of the project. As the game was initially developed for PC, my main role was to port it to the console platforms and ensure the game runs smoothly on those platforms. The Nintendo Switch port development was especially challenging due to hardware limitations.

Household Mecha

Platform: PC
Status: Released
Engine: Unity
Team: 1 programmer, 1 3D artist
Role: Gameplay programmer

About:
Household Mecha is a third person 3D puzzle platformer game. You play as a little toy robot whose dream is to one day become big enough to fight kaijus it saw on TV. The objective is to maneuver around the house to collect and attach as many items to yourself as possible to grow big enough to leave the house.

Comments:
Household Mecha was a game jam project developed within 48 hours. It was submitted for the GMTK Game Jam in 2021. As this was a game jam, it was quite challenging trying to get things done within the time constraint. We finished the implementation of the basic core gameplay, but ran out of time to implement a proper ending for the poor robot 😢

Slice of Life

Platform: PC
Status: Released
Engine: Unreal Engine 4
Team: 1 programmer, 2 designers, 3 3D artists
Role: Gameplay programmer

About:
Slice of Life is an arcade-like monster creation simulation game on. The objective is to go around each level collecting different body parts and assembling them to make your own unique monster. Your monster creation is then submitted to the customer and rated at the end of each level. The more satisfied your customers are with your creations, the better your rewards. The game was uploaded to Itch.io to download and play for free.

Comments:
Slice of Life was my final year major project at college. The project lasted for almost 7 months, making it the largest project I have ever worked on at the time. I worked on almost all the core gameplay functionalities, with a little bit of help on the UI side from the designers. Due to time constraints, there were many features that had to be omitted from the original vision, but it was a project we were proud to graduate with nonetheless.

VR Carnival

Platform: PC
Status: Completed
Engine: Unity
Team: 1 programmer, 2 designers, 3 3D artists
Role: Gameplay programmer

About:
VR Carnival is a VR game where you throw darts at a board to pop balloons. The balloons come in different sizes and points - the smaller ones are worth more points. The player's objective is to score as many points using the limited number of darts per round. The player can then spend their points to unlock prizes.

Comments:
VR Carnival was a short 2 weeks college project done for the purpose of exploring VR development. I created all the core gameplay functionalities, including the dart throwing, the points system and the prize system.

Island Defense

Platform: PC
Status: Completed
Engine: Unity
Team: 1 programmer, 1 designer and 4 3D artists
Role: Gameplay programmer

About:
Island Defense is a game where you try to defend your floating island tower against the endless assault of enemies.

Comments:
This was a college game jam project made in 24 hours spread across 3 days. I created most of the functionalities, including the tower controls and enemy spawner. The original plan was to allow the players to spend points for upgrades after each wave, but this feature was not implemented due to time constraints.
